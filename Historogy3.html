Model answer (short):

The skin consists of three main layers: the epidermis (outer protective keratinized layer containing melanocytes, Langerhans and Merkel cells), the dermis (connective tissue with blood vessels, nerves, hair follicles and glands) and the hypodermis (subcutaneous fat and connective tissue). Functions include protection against mechanical and microbial injury and UV radiation, thermoregulation (via blood flow and sweat), vitamin D synthesis, sensation, and excretion of small amounts of waste through sweat.

Model answer (short):

Melanin is produced by melanocytes using the enzyme tyrosinase, converting tyrosine to dopa and then to melanin. Melanin protects epidermal cells from UV-induced DNA damage and determines skin and hair color. Disorders include albinism (lack of tyrosinase → no melanin), vitiligo (loss of melanocytes → depigmented patches), and hyperpigmentation conditions. Melanin also influences susceptibility to skin cancers; low melanin increases risk.
