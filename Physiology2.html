Essay Questions — 2 questions

1. Describe the steps of oxidative phosphorylation, naming the main complexes (I–IV), where protons are pumped, and how ATP synthase uses the proton gradient to make ATP. (Expected length: ~250–350 words)

Expected points / marking rubric:

  • Describe electron flow from NADH/FADH2 through Complexes I–IV (3 marks)
  • State which complexes pump protons (I, III, IV) and where protons accumulate (intermembrane space) (3 marks)
  • Explain proton motive force and ATP synthase mechanism (F0F1 rotation) (3 marks)
  • Mention role of oxygen as final electron acceptor and water formation (1 mark)
  • Clarity, organization and correct terminology (up to 2 marks)
2. Compare and contrast the three cytoskeletal elements (microtubules, microfilaments/actin, and intermediate filaments) in terms of structure, diameter, primary functions, and associated motor proteins. (Expected length: ~200–300 words)

Expected points / marking rubric:

  • Describe structural subunits and diameters (microtubules ~25 nm, actin ~7 nm, IFs 8–12 nm) (3 marks)
  • List main functions: transport/mitotic spindle (MT), contraction/cell cortex (actin), mechanical strength (IF) (3 marks)
  • Mention motor proteins (kinesin/dynein for MT, myosin for actin) (2 marks)
  • Provide examples of cell processes relying on each and clarity (2 marks)
