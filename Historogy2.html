Topic: Epithelial Tissue

Essay Questions

1. Describe the structure and functions of epithelial tissue.

Show Expected Answer

Epithelial tissue is composed of tightly packed cells with minimal extracellular material. It covers body surfaces, lines organs, and forms glands. The cells have polarity (apical, lateral, basal surfaces) and rest on a basement membrane. Functions include protection, absorption, secretion, sensation, and contractility.

2. Explain the different types of epithelial cells based on their shape and layers, giving examples of each.

Show Expected Answer

Epithelia are classified by the number of layers and cell shape. Simple epithelia (one layer) include simple squamous (lungs), simple cuboidal (kidney tubules), and simple columnar (intestines). Stratified epithelia (many layers) include stratified squamous (skin), and transitional (urinary bladder). Pseudostratified epithelium (trachea) appears multilayered but is single-layered.
