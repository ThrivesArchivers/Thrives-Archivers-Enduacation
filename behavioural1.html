Multiple Choice (20)

1. Which discipline lies at the heart of behavioural sciences?
Correct — Psychology.
Psychology is the central discipline in behavioural sciences because it focuses on behaviour and mental processes.
2. Which school of thought emphasises observable behaviour and rewards/punishments?
Correct — Behaviourism.
Behaviourism focuses on learning through reinforcement and avoids studying private mental processes.
3. Which tradition introduced the idea of the unconscious influencing behaviour?
Correct — Psychoanalysis.
Psychoanalytic theory (e.g., Freud) emphasises unconscious memories and motives shaping behaviour.
4. "The whole is greater than the sum of its parts" is a central idea in which perspective?
Correct — Gestalt psychology.
Gestalt psychologists emphasise perception of organized wholes rather than isolated elements.
5. Bandura's Social Learning Theory emphasises learning through:
Correct — Observational learning.
Bandura proposed that people learn by observing others and internalising behaviours, not just by external rewards.
6. Which field studies how social factors affect illness and recovery (as stated in the course aim)?
Correct — Behavioural Sciences.
The course aims to help students understand how social factors influence illness and recovery.
7. Which of the following is NOT listed by Leary (2008) as part of behavioural sciences?
Correct — Astrophysics.
Leary listed Psychology, Sociology, Communication, Human development, Education, Marketing, Social Work, etc.; astrophysics is a natural science, not behavioural.
8. Which perspective emphasises free will and human potential (Carl Rogers)?
Correct — Humanistic psychology.
Humanistic psychology emphasises free will, self-determination and the positive potential of people.
9. Which of these is an example of a "mental process"?
Correct — Memory.
Mental processes include private phenomena like memory, thoughts, perceptions and feelings.
10. Cultural psychology distinguishes between 'etics' and 'emics'. 'Etics' refers to:
Correct — Etics = universal processes.
Etics are universal processes (e.g., capacity to learn language); emics are culture-specific expressions.
11. According to the slides, why is behavioural science important in medicine?
Correct — behaviours influence health.
Behaviours such as smoking contribute to disease; psychological factors affect outcomes and recovery.
12. The slides state that doctors are at risk for which of the following due to the stresses of medical practice?
Correct — higher stress and burnout.
Medical practice is emotionally and behaviourally demanding and linked with higher levels of distress among doctors.
13. Which topic would you expect to find in a psychology textbook chapter described in the slides?
Correct — Biological basis of behaviour.
Psychology covers interfaces with biological sciences like neurons, hormones and the nervous system.
14. If a behaviour was previously punished, behaviourist theory predicts that it will:
Correct — less likely to reoccur.
Punishment reduces the likelihood of behaviour repeating according to behaviourist principles.
15. An example of an observable behaviour is:
Correct — Crying.
Behaviours that others can directly observe (e.g., crying, laughing) are central to behavioural science observation.
16. Which method is essential for behavioural scientists to draw valid conclusions about behaviour?
Correct — scientific method.
Scientists use observation, hypotheses and testing to confirm conclusions about behaviour.
17. Which of the following would be considered a cultural (emics) influence?
Correct — specific language.
Emics refer to culture-specific expressions (e.g., which language is used), while etics are universal abilities.
18. Which of these topics does NOT belong under behavioural sciences as used in the course slides?
Correct — Quantum mechanics.
Quantum mechanics is a physical science, not listed by Leary among behavioural science disciplines.
19. Which example from the slides is used to show an unconscious slip in speech?
Correct — Freudian slip.
A Freudian slip is an example of behaviour influenced by unconscious material.
20. The course requires attendance and studying outside class because:
Correct — Attend and study.
The lecturer emphasises that slides complement lectures; attending class and personal study are necessary for success.

True / False (10)

1. Behavioural sciences include communication and social work. (True/False)
Correct — True.
Leary lists communication and social work among behavioural sciences.
2. Humanistic psychology denies free will and personal growth. (True/False)
Correct — False.
Humanistic psychology emphasises free will and human potential.
3. The unconscious is a concept central to behaviourist theory. (True/False)
Correct — False.
The unconscious is central to psychoanalysis, not behaviourism; behaviourism focuses on observable behaviour.
4. Observational learning means people can learn by watching others. (True/False)
Correct — True.
Social learning theory highlights learning through observation and modelling.
5. A Freudian slip can reflect unconscious thoughts. (True/False)
Correct — True.
Freudian slips are examples of behaviour influenced by unconscious material.
6. The course suggests slides alone are sufficient to replace attending class. (True/False)
Correct — False.
The lecturer emphasises that reading slides is no substitute for attending class.
7. Behavioural scientists can infer mental processes by observing behaviour. (True/False)
Correct — True.
Because mental processes are private, scientists infer them from observable behaviour.
8. Etics are culture-specific. (True/False)
Correct — False.
Etics refer to universal processes; emics are culture-specific.
9. Behavioural sciences are irrelevant to health promotion. (True/False)
Correct — False.
Behavioural sciences are key to understanding behaviour change and health promotion.
10. The study of neurons and hormones is unrelated to psychology. (True/False)
Correct — False.
Psychology interfaces with biological sciences; neurons and hormones are important for behaviour.
