Essay 1
Explain the main cellular components of connective tissue and their functions. (Write your answer below)
Model answer (short):

Major resident cells: fibroblasts (synthesize collagen, elastic fibers, and ground substance; when inactive called fibrocytes), adipocytes (store triglycerides, secrete leptin, insulate and cushion), reticular cells (produce reticular fibers). Major migratory cells: macrophages (phagocytose debris and pathogens), plasma cells (produce antibodies), mast cells (release histamine and mediate inflammation), and leukocytes (neutrophils, eosinophils) that respond to infection. Each cell contributes to structure, defence, repair, or metabolic regulation.

Essay 2
Compare dense regular, dense irregular and loose connective tissue in terms of fiber arrangement and typical function/locations.
Model answer (short):

Dense regular CT: collagen fibers aligned parallel (tendons, ligaments) — resists tension in one direction. Dense irregular CT: bundles of collagen randomly interwoven (dermis, organ capsules) — resists stresses from multiple directions. Loose (areolar) CT: fibers scattered with abundant ground substance and many cells (beneath epithelia, between muscles) — supports, holds fluids, and provides immune defense and nutrition.
